Unemployment Insurance Estimator

This site allows you to estimate how much your Oregon Unemployment Insurance Benefits will be before you file a new unemployment insurance claim. If you are receiving, or have received benefits on an existing claim, that claim must expire before a new claim can be filed.

For a detailed description of how the weekly benefit amount is calculated, please see the Unemployment Insurance Claimant Handbook available at: How to Apply for Unemployment Insurance Benefits.

Please enter the gross wages (amounts earned before taxes were withheld) paid to you in the quarters indicated below. If you were not paid wages during a given quarter, enter zero for that quarter. When entering your wages, do not enter dollar signs, decimal points, or cents.

You may use pay stubs and/or W2 forms to determine your wages. If you use the wage information on a W2 form, convert the yearly amount reported into quarterly amounts. Include wages paid for work performed in another state, for the federal government and for the military.

Note: This is an estimate only, based on the information you provide. Your actual benefit amount may be different.
